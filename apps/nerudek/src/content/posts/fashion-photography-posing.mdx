# Fashion Photography Posing Techniques for Editorial Shoots

Great fashion photography is a collaboration between photographer, stylist, and model. While lighting and composition matter enormously, the pose is what sells the garment and tells the story. This guide covers foundational posing techniques used on editorial sets worldwide.

## The Triangle Rule

The human eye is drawn to shapes. By bending elbows, knees, and tilting the head, a model creates **triangular negative space** that adds dynamism to a static frame. Avoid straight vertical lines — they flatten the image.

## Body Angle and Weight Distribution

- **Three-quarter turn**: Standing at a 45-degree angle to the camera slims the silhouette and adds depth.
- **Weight on back leg**: Shifting weight to the rear leg creates a subtle hip pop and an S-curve through the torso.
- **Leading with the shoulder**: Dropping one shoulder lower than the other introduces asymmetry and movement.

## Facial Expression Control

Editorial fashion often calls for **neutral intensity** rather than a broad smile. Techniques include:

- **Soft gaze**: Relax the eyes and look slightly past the lens to avoid stare-down intensity.
- **Micro-expressions**: A barely-there smirk or parted lips can convey confidence without theatrics.
- **Jaw forward and down**: Extending the chin slightly elongates the neck and defines the jawline.

## Hand Placement

Hands are notoriously difficult. Common solutions:

1. **Light touch**: Fingers resting gently on the face, collarbone, or hip — never pressing hard.
2. **Floating hands**: Hands in motion, adjusting hair or a garment, feel natural.
3. **Invisible hands**: Pockets, behind the back, or cropped out of frame when they distract.

## Movement and Posing

Static poses can feel rigid. Adding micro-movement — a hair toss, a weight shift, a blink — gives the photographer more frames to choose from and often yields the most organic shot.

Mastering these techniques takes repetition, but the result is a portfolio that feels professional, editorial, and alive.
